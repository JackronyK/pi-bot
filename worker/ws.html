Let's evaluate the given series step-by-step:

Step 1: Identify the type of series

The given series is: 1 + 1/7 + 1/49 + 1/343 + ...

Let's look at the relationship between consecutive terms:

  • From the 1st term (1) to the 2nd term (1/7), we multiply by 1/7.
  • From the 2nd term (1/7) to the 3rd term (1/49), we multiply by 1/7 (since (1/7) * (1/7) = 1/49).
  • From the 3rd term (1/49) to the 4th term (1/343), we multiply by 1/7 (since (1/49) * (1/7) = 1/343).

Since each term is obtained by multiplying the previous term by a constant value, this is an infinite geometric series.

Step 2: Determine the first term (a) and common ratio (r)

In a geometric series:

  • The first term (a) is the first number in the series. Here, a = 1.
  • The common ratio (r) is the constant value by which each term is multiplied to get the next term. Here, r = 1/7.

Step 3: Check the condition for the sum of an infinite geometric series

An infinite geometric series has a finite sum only if the absolute value of the common ratio (|r|) is less than 1 (|r| < 1).

In our case, r = 1/7. So, |1/7| = 1/7.

Since 1/7 < 1, the condition is met, and we can find the sum of this series.

Step 4: Apply the formula for the sum of an infinite geometric series

The formula for the sum (S) of an infinite geometric series is:

S = a / (1 - r)

Step 5: Substitute the values and calculate the sum

Substitute a = 1 and r = 1/7 into the formula:

S = 1 / (1 - 1/7)

First, calculate the denominator:

1 - 1/7 = 7/7 - 1/7 = 6/7

Now, substitute this back into the formula for S:

S = 1 / (6/7)

Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal:

S = 1 * (7/6)

S = 7/6

As a decimal, 7/6 is approximately 1.166666...

Conclusion:

The sum of the infinite geometric series 1 + 1/7 + 1/49 + 1/343 + ... is 7/6.
